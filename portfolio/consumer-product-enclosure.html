Consumer Product Enclosure (2024)

Designing an ergonomic and aesthetically pleasing enclosure for a new consumer electronic device, optimized for mass production and user experience.

Project Overview

This project focused on the industrial design and mechanical engineering of an enclosure for a cutting-edge consumer electronic product. Key considerations included user ergonomics, aesthetic appeal, ease of assembly, and cost-effective manufacturing methods suitable for high-volume production.

We conducted extensive user research to inform the ergonomic design, followed by iterative prototyping and testing. Material selection was crucial to balance durability, weight, and tactile feel. The design was meticulously optimized for injection molding, ensuring minimal material usage and cycle times.

Key Challenges & Solutions

  • Ergonomics & Aesthetics: Developed multiple design iterations based on user feedback and industrial design principles, achieving a sleek and comfortable form factor.
  • Mass Production Optimization: Designed for injection molding with careful consideration of draft, ribs, snap-fits, and gate locations to minimize tooling costs and production time.
  • Component Integration: Ensured precise fit and secure mounting for all internal electronic components, including PCBs, batteries, and connectors.
