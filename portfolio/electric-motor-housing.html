Electric Motor Housing (2024)

A detailed look into the design and optimization of an electric motor housing, focusing on thermal management and manufacturing efficiency.

Project Overview

This project involved the complete mechanical design of an electric motor housing. The primary goals were to ensure efficient heat dissipation, optimize for die-casting manufacturing processes, and facilitate easy assembly of internal components.

We utilized advanced CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) simulations to analyze airflow and heat transfer, ensuring optimal cooling performance under various operating conditions. Structural analysis (FEA) was also performed to guarantee the housing's integrity and durability.

Key Challenges & Solutions

  • Thermal Management: Designed internal fins and external geometries to maximize surface area for heat exchange, validated with CFD.
  • Manufacturing Optimization: Collaborated closely with die-casting specialists to ensure draft angles, wall thicknesses, and parting lines were suitable for high-volume production.
  • Assembly Efficiency: Developed a modular design that simplified the assembly process, reducing production time and potential errors.
